# Final Night Revision Sheet (High-Yield Points)

> [!NOTE]
> Read these 10 core concepts 2 hours before the examination to lock in key definitions and details.

1. **Letter of Credit (L/C)**: A document issued by the buyer's bank guaranteeing payment to the exporter upon receipt of specific shipping documents. Eliminates non-payment risk.
2. **Bill of Lading**: Legal document issued by carrier to shipper detailing cargo type, destination. Acts as receipt, contract of carriage, and title document.
3. **Porter's Diamond Model**: 4 national attributes explaining country competitive advantage: Factor Conditions, Demand Conditions, Related/Supporting Industries, and Firm Strategy/Rivalry.
4. **Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions**: Framework for analyzing cross-cultural communication: Power Distance, Individualism, Masculinity, Uncertainty Avoidance, Long-term Orientation.
5. **Special Drawing Rights (SDR)**: An international reserve asset created by the IMF, valued based on a basket of five currencies (USD, Euro, RMB, Yen, Pound).
6. **Incoterms**: 11 shipping terms defining responsibilities (e.g. DDP: Delivered Duty Paid, FOB: Free On Board).
7. **Expatriate Failure**: Expatriate manager returning prematurely from foreign posting. Main cause: inability of family/spouse to adapt.
8. **Dumping**: Selling a product in a foreign market at a price lower than its cost of production or fair market value.
9. **Heckscher-Ohlin Theory**: A country exports goods that use its abundant factor of production intensively (e.g. labor-rich countries export labor-intensive goods).
10. **Gold Standard**: Monetary system where currencies are pegged to a specific amount of gold. Created stable exchange rates but lacked monetary policy flexibility.
