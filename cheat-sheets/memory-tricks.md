# Master Cheat Sheet: Mnemonics and Exam Memory Tricks

Use these mnemonics to recall complex academic lists and structures during LPU examinations.

---

## 1. PESTLE Framework (Unit 1: Environment Analysis)
- **Concept**: The factors of the macro-environment.
- **Mnemonic**: **P-E-S-T-L-E**
  - **P**: Political (government stability, policies)
  - **E**: Economic (exchange rates, GDP, interest rates)
  - **S**: Social (cultural demographics, consumer tastes)
  - **T**: Technological (AI, digital trade, R&D)
  - **L**: Legal (employment laws, IP protection)
  - **E**: Environmental (sustainability, climate change)

---

## 2. EPG Profile (Unit 6: Global HRM Staffing Policies)
- **Concept**: Strategic staffing orientations.
- **Mnemonic**: **E-P-G**
  - **E**: Ethnocentric (parent country managers run foreign units)
  - **P**: Polycentric (host country managers run local units)
  - **G**: Geocentric (best managers hired regardless of passport)

---

## 3. Four Entry Strategies (Unit 4: Strategy of International Business)
- **Concept**: Pressures for cost reduction vs local responsiveness.
- **Mnemonic**: **G-L-I-T**
  - **G**: Global Standardization (low cost focus, standardized product)
  - **L**: Localization (adapted product, high local responsiveness)
  - **I**: International (transferring core capabilities, low cost & local pressure)
  - **T**: Transnational (balancing both cost reduction and high localization)

---

## 4. Porter's Diamond Model Attributes (Unit 2: Trade Theory)
- **Concept**: Four primary elements of competitive advantage.
- **Mnemonic**: **F-D-R-S**
  - **F**: Factor Conditions (labor, capital)
  - **D**: Demand Conditions (sophisticated buyers)
  - **R**: Related and Supporting Industries (supply networks)
  - **S**: Strategy, Structure, and Rivalry (intensity of local competition)
