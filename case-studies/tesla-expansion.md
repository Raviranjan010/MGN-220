# Case Study: Tesla's Global Expansion & FDI Strategy

## 1. Background & Context
Tesla Inc. has transformed from a niche electric vehicle (EV) startup into the world's most valuable automaker. A key driver of this scale has been its foreign expansion through Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in manufacturing facilities, known as "Gigafactories."

```
                   [ Tesla FDI Strategy ]
                              │
         ┌────────────────────┼────────────────────┐
         ▼                    ▼                    ▼
   [ Shanghai, China ]   [ Berlin, Germany ]   [ Bangalore, India ]
   (Wholly Owned WOS)    (European Presence)   (R&D & Market Entry)
```

## 2. Foreign Direct Investment Modes
Tesla's expansion uses **Wholly Owned Subsidiaries (WOS)** via **Greenfield Investments** (building factories from scratch):
- **Gigafactory Shanghai (China)**: Established in 2019. Tesla was the first foreign automaker allowed to own a manufacturing plant in China without a local joint-venture partner.
- **Gigafactory Berlin-Brandenburg (Germany)**: Established to serve the European market, bypassing tariffs and transport costs.
- **Gigafactory Texas & Nevada (USA)**: Core domestic manufacturing.

## 3. Strategic Rationale for Greenfield WOS
- **IP Protection**: Tesla has proprietary battery, motor, and autonomous driving technology. A WOS ensures complete control over IP without sharing it with a joint venture partner.
- **Operational Control**: Greenfield allows Tesla to design advanced, highly automated manufacturing layouts (e.g., using massive casting machines) from scratch, which is impossible in pre-existing acquired facilities.
- **Government Incentives**: Direct negotiation with local governments (like Shanghai) secured cheap land, tax holidays, and fast-tracked approvals.

## 4. Exam-Oriented Analysis & Solved Questions

### Q1: Why did Tesla prefer Greenfield FDI over Joint Ventures or Licensing? (5 Marks)
* **Topper's Answer**:
  - **Protection of Core Technology**: Licensing or Joint Ventures create a high risk of technology leakage (know-how dissemination risk).
  - **Operational Synergy**: Under Greenfield, Tesla implements its customized "factory-as-a-product" layout.
  - **Profit Appropriation**: Tesla retains 100% of the profits from the highly lucrative Chinese and European markets, rather than splitting margins with partners.

### Q2: Discuss the host country benefits and costs of Tesla's Gigafactory Shanghai for China. (5 Marks)
* **Topper's Answer**:
  - **Benefits to China (Host Country)**:
    - **Resource Transfer**: Introduction of state-of-the-art EV assembly tech.
    - **Employment Effects**: Creation of thousands of manufacturing and engineering jobs.
    - **Balance of Payments**: China exports Shanghai-made Tesla cars to Europe, improving its trade balance.
  - **Costs to China**:
    - **Competition**: Puts pressure on local EV makers (BYD, Nio, Xpeng) to survive.
    - **Capital Outflow**: Profits generated by Tesla will eventually be repatriated back to the US parent company.
