## 🔑 SECTION 1: KEY POINTS — CORE CURRICULUM

### ▶ Global Marketing & R&D
*   **Global Marketing**: Sourcing, pricing, promoting, and distributing goods in international markets.
*   **Standardization**: Selling identical products with identical marketing campaigns globally. Lower costs, brand consistency (e.g., Apple).
*   **Adaptation**: Customizing the 4Ps for local cultures, incomes, and regulations. Higher sales, high setup costs (e.g., McDonald's India).
*   **Price Discrimination**: Charging different prices in different markets based on demand elasticity (e.g., lower textbook prices in developing countries).
*   **Price Escalation**: Retail price increase in foreign markets due to tariffs, shipping, and local middlemen margins.
*   **Retail Concentration**: Concentrated retail (few large stores, e.g., US) leads to short channels. Fragmented retail (millions of small stores, e.g., India) leads to long channels.
*   **R&D Mismatches**: Fails if R&D does not coordinate with Manufacturing (too expensive to build) or Marketing (consumers do not want it).

### ▶ Global Human Resource Management (GHRM)
*   **PCNs (Parent-Country Nationals)**: Citizens of the HQ country working at a foreign unit (e.g., Japanese manager in US Toyota plant).
*   **HCNs (Host-Country Nationals)**: Citizens of the local country working at the local unit (e.g., US worker in US Toyota plant).
*   **TCNs (Third-Country Nationals)**: Citizens of a third country working at a foreign unit (e.g., French manager in US Toyota plant).
*   **Perlmutter's EPG Staffing Framework**:
    *   **Ethnocentric**: Key subsidiary roles held by PCNs. Fit: International strategy. Risk: Cultural myopia.
    *   **Polycentric**: Subsidiaries managed by HCNs. Fit: Localization strategy. Risk: HQ-subsidiary communication gap.
    *   **Geocentric**: Hires the best candidate regardless of nationality. Fit: Transnational strategy. Risk: High cost and immigration visas.
*   **Expatriate Failure**: Early return of an expat manager due to performance issues. Primary cause: Spouse/family inability to adjust to host culture.
*   **Repatriation**: Reintegrating the expat back into the home organization. Fails if returned manager is demoted or international skills are ignored.

## 🔑 SECTION 2: MEMORY ACROBATS & STUDY AIDS

### 💡 Staffing Policies Orientation Matrix (Perlmutter)
*   **Ethnocentric** $\rightarrow$ **HQ Oriented** (PCNs run the show; corporate culture consistency).
*   **Polycentric** $\rightarrow$ **Host Oriented** (HCNs run subsidiaries; high local cultural fit).
*   **Geocentric** $\rightarrow$ **Global Oriented** (Best manager runs the office; transnational integration).

### 💡 Standardization vs. Adaptation Spectrum
```
   [ STANDARDIZATION ] ◄─────────────────────────────────────────────► [ ADAPTATION ]
  • Cost Focus (Scale economies)                                     • Sales Focus (Customer fit)
  • Identical product & positioning                                  • Modified 4Ps by country
  • Unified global brand image                                       • Multi-brand regional setups
  • Best for: Microchips, Luxury goods                               • Best for: Food, Consumer retail
```

## 🎯 EXAM TIPS FOR 100% MARKS

### ✅ How to Tackle Staffing Case Questions
1.  **Analyze Strategy Fit**:
    *   If the case MNC uses a **Global Standardization** or **International** strategy, recommend **Ethnocentric** staffing to protect core corporate competencies.
    *   If the case MNC uses a **Localization** strategy (like Heinz or McDonald's), recommend **Polycentric** staffing to leverage local managers.
    *   If the case MNC uses a **Transnational** strategy (like Samsung), recommend **Geocentric** staffing to build a global cadre.
2.  **Highlight the Family Element**: If asked about expatriate failure, always point out that **spouse adjustment** is the #1 statistical cause of failure. Do not focus solely on the manager's technical work.
3.  **Discuss Repatriation Proactively**: Emphasize that GHRM does not end when the expat arrives overseas; the firm must have a clear repatriation plan to retain the returned manager's international experience.

### ✅ Common Exam Mistakes to Avoid
*   ❌ **Confusing PCNs, HCNs, and TCNs**: Make sure you identify them correctly:
    *   *PCN* is from the home country.
    *   *HCN* is from the host country.
    *   *TCN* is from a completely neutral third country.
*   ❌ **Overlooking R&D Mismatch**: When discussing global R&D, do not just explain technology. You must mention **cross-functional integration** between R&D, Marketing, and Manufacturing to prevent market failures.
*   ❌ **Assuming Geocentric is Always Best**: Do not say geocentric is the perfect policy. Critically evaluate it: it is highly expensive, faces massive visa/immigration barriers, and requires standardizing global salary grades, which is highly complex.
*   ❌ **Dumping Definition confusion**: Do not confuse dumping with price discrimination. Price discrimination is charging different prices based on market demand. Dumping is selling *below cost of production* to wipe out competitors.
