# Comparison Tables Matrix (Full Syllabus)

## 1. Absolute Advantage vs. Comparative Advantage

| Attribute | Absolute Advantage (Adam Smith) | Comparative Advantage (David Ricardo) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Absolute productivity differences. | Relative opportunity cost differences. |
| **Principle** | Produce goods you make more efficiently than anyone else. | Produce goods you make at a lower opportunity cost relative to others. |
| **Feasibility** | If a country is worse at making all goods, trade cannot happen. | Trade is always beneficial if relative opportunity costs differ. |

## 2. GATT vs. WTO

| Attribute | GATT (General Agreement on Tariffs/Trade) | WTO (World Trade Organization) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nature** | A multilateral treaty with no institutional structure. | A permanent international institution. |
| **Scope** | Covered trade in goods only. | Covers trade in goods, services (GATS), and intellectual property (TRIPS). |
| **Dispute Resolution**| Slow, required unanimous consent of members. | Fast, binding rulings by Dispute Settlement Body. |

## 3. IMF vs. World Bank

| Attribute | IMF (International Monetary Fund) | World Bank Group |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Role** | Maintains exchange rate stability and monetary order. | Promotes economic development and poverty reduction. |
| **Lending Type** | Short-term loans for balance-of-payments stabilization. | Long-term loans for developmental/infrastructure projects. |
| **Target** | Central banks and macroeconomic policies. | Infrastructure projects (roads, solar grids, health). |
