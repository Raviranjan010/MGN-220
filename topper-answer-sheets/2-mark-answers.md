# Topper Solved Answers: 2-Mark Questions (Short, Direct & Structured)

> [!TIP]
> **Exam Strategy**: Keep answers under 30-50 words. State the definition clearly and provide one example or formula. Always write in bullet points.

---

### Q1: Define 'Mercantilism' in international trade.
**Topper's Answer**:
- **Definition**: An 16th-century economic theory stating that a nation's wealth depends on accumulating gold and silver.
- **Core Principle**: A country should maximize exports (surplus) and minimize imports (deficit) through government intervention.
- **Example**: British trade colonial policies prior to the American Revolution.

---

### Q2: What is the main difference between Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) and Foreign Portfolio Investment (FPI)?
**Topper's Answer**:

| Feature | FDI | FPI |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Control** | Direct ownership and management control (>10% equity). | Financial investment without operational control (<10% equity). |
| **Nature** | Long-term physical assets (e.g., factories). | Short-term liquid assets (e.g., stocks, bonds). |

---

### Q3: Define 'Bill of Lading'.
**Topper's Answer**:
- **Definition**: A legal document issued by a carrier to a shipper detailing the type, quantity, and destination of goods being carried.
- **Key Roles**: It acts as:
  1. A contract of carriage.
  2. A receipt of cargo.
  3. A document of title (can be traded).

---

### Q4: Explain 'Expatriate Failure'.
**Topper's Answer**:
- **Definition**: The premature return of an expatriate manager (employee sent abroad) to their home country due to inability to perform or adjust.
- **Primary Cause**: Inability of the spouse or family to adapt to the host country's cultural/social environment.

---

### Q5: Define 'Arbitrage' in foreign exchange.
**Topper's Answer**:
- **Definition**: The simultaneous purchase and sale of an asset (currency) in different markets to exploit price discrepancies for risk-free profit.
- **Example**: Buying Euros in London at $1.10 and immediately selling them in New York at $1.11.
