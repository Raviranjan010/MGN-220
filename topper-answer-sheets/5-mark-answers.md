### Q1: Explain the five levels of Regional Economic Integration with a diagram.
**Topper's Answer**:
- **Definition**: Agreements between countries in a geographic region to reduce tariff and non-tariff barriers to the free flow of goods, services, and factors of production.
- **The Five Levels (from lowest to highest)**:
  1. **Free Trade Area (FTA)**: Tariffs removed among members, but countries maintain independent external trade policies (e.g., USMCA/NAFTA).
  2. **Customs Union**: FTA + common external trade policy toward non-members (e.g., MERCOSUR).
  3. **Common Market**: Customs Union + free movement of factors of production (labor, capital) (e.g., European Common Market historically).
  4. **Economic Union**: Common Market + common currency, harmonized tax rates, and monetary policy (e.g., Eurozone).
  5. **Political Union**: Full economic integration + unified political and governance structure (e.g., United States).

- **Diagram**:
```
[Political Union] ◄── [Economic Union] ◄── [Common Market] ◄── [Customs Union] ◄── [Free Trade Area]
(Highest integration)                                                                (Lowest integration)
```

### Q2: Compare Ethnocentric, Polycentric, and Geocentric staffing policies in Global HRM.
**Topper's Answer**:
- **Definition**: Staffing policies determine how an MNC selects, recruits, and places managers in its foreign subsidiaries.

| Policy | Description | Key Advantage | Key Disadvantage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ethnocentric** | Key positions held by Parent-Country Nationals (PCNs). | Protects core competencies; aligned corporate culture. | Leads to cultural myopia; high expatriate failure costs. |
| **Polycentric** | Subsidiary run by Host-Country Nationals (HCNs). | Eliminates cultural adjustment barriers; lower cost. | Gap between HQ and subsidiary; career path limitations. |
| **Geocentric** | Best managers hired regardless of nationality. | Creates global executive cadre; high learning curve. | Very expensive; immigration policy constraints. |

- **Mnemonic**: **EPG** (Ethnocentric, Polycentric, Geocentric).
- **Corporate Example**: **Apple** uses a Geocentric model at HQ and localized polycentric management at regional retail branches.
