# Unit 1 — Topper Model Answers

## Q: Critically analyze how political risk affects FDI entry modes. (10 Marks)
- **Definition**: Political risk is the likelihood that political decisions/events will impact a firm's profitability.
- **Answer Structure**:
  - *Introduction*: Introduce political risk types (micro vs macro risk).
  - *Impact on Entry Modes*:
    - **High Risk**: Avoid Wholly Owned Subsidiaries (Greenfield). Prefer exporting, licensing, or joint ventures with local partners to share risk.
    - **Low Risk**: Greenfield FDI is preferred to protect technology and maintain control.
  - *Example*: Tesla entering China via a wholly owned factory once foreign ownership regulations were relaxed.
  - *Conclusion*: Risk mitigation through political insurance (MIGA) and local integration.
