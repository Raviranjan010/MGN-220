# Unit 1 — International Business Environment: Detailed Notes

## Topic: Introduction to International Business
### 1. Introduction
International Business (IB) refers to the trade of goods, services, technology, capital, and/or knowledge across national borders.

### 2. Simple Beginner Explanation
If you sell cookies to your neighbor, it's domestic. If you mail cookies to France and receive Euros, you are doing International Business! It involves crossing borders, dealing with different currencies, languages, and tastes.

### 3. Formal Academic Definition
> **Academic Definition:**
> "International Business consists of all commercial transactions—including sales, investments, and transportation—that take place between two or more countries." — John Daniels

### 4. Key Features & Characteristics
- **Cross-Border Transactions**: Crosses national boundaries.
- **Multiple Currencies**: Involves exchange rate risk.
- **PESTLE Diversity**: Interacting with multiple cultures and laws.
- **High Risk**: Exposure to political instability and currency shifts.

### 5. Advantages & Disadvantages
- **Advantages**: Sales expansion, resource acquisition, risk diversification.
- **Disadvantages**: Higher tariffs, cultural errors, exchange rate losses.

### 6. Real-World Business Example
- **McDonald's**: Operates in 100+ countries, adapting its menu (e.g. McAloo Tikki in India) while keeping operations standardized.

### 7. AI Applications
- AI tools predict shipping customs delays and translate contract languages instantly.

## Topic: Globalization
### 1. Introduction
Globalization is the increasing integration and interdependence of national economies.

### 2. Drivers of Globalization
- **Declining Trade Barriers**: Tariffs dropping since WWII (WTO.org).
- **Technological Advances**: Containerization, the Internet, and AI.

### 3. Solved Case / Analogies
- *Analogy*: Sourcing ingredients for a pizza from 5 different countries.
- *Corporate Case*: **Apple Inc.** designs in California, sources chips from Taiwan, and assembles in China/India.

## Topic: PESTLE Analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Tech, Legal, Environmental)
### 1. Concept Summary
Firms analyze the external macro-environment to make market entry decisions.
- **Political/Legal**: Legal systems (Common, Civil, Theocratic).
- **Social/Cultural**: Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions.
- **AI Application**: AI-powered macro-risk monitoring tools.

### 2. Difference Table: Domestic vs. International Environment

| Feature | Domestic Environment | International Environment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Culture** | Homogeneous and familiar. | Heterogeneous and diverse. |
| **Legal System**| Single jurisdiction. | Multiple complex jurisdictions. |
| **Currency** | One domestic currency. | Multiple fluctuating currencies. |
