## 🔑 SECTION 1: KEY POINTS — CORE CURRICULUM

### ▶ Global Monetary System & Forex Market
*   **Global Monetary System**: Rules, institutions, and agreements regulating international financial transactions and exchange rates.
*   **Forex Market**: Decentralized, global, over-the-counter market for buying and selling currencies. Daily volume exceeds $7.5 trillion.
*   **Forex Functions**: Currency conversion, short-term trade credit, and exchange rate insurance (hedging).
*   **Forex Participants**: Commercial/investment banks, MNCs, central banks, speculators, and arbitrageurs.
*   **Exchange Rate**: The value of one currency in terms of another currency (e.g., 1 USD = 83.50 INR).
*   **Exchange Rate Determinants**: Inflation, interest rates, balance of payments (current account deficits), public debt, terms of trade, and political stability.

### ▶ International Monetary System (IMS) & Evolution
*   **IMS**: Rules and institutions governing international currency payments.
*   **Gold Standard (1870s – 1939)**: Currencies pegged to gold. Fixed, stable exchange rates. Suspended during WWI, collapsed in the Great Depression.
*   **Bretton Woods System (1944 – 1973)**: Currencies pegged to the USD; USD convertible to gold at $35/ounce. Fixed exchange rates with 1% trading bands and IMF adjustment approvals. Collapsed in 1971 due to inflation and USD supply excesses (Nixon Shock).
*   **Floating Exchange Rates (1973 – Present)**: Currencies float freely based on supply and demand, with occasional central bank interventions (managed float).

### ▶ International Financial Institutions (IFIs)
*   **IMF**: Lender of last resort. Monitors economies (surveillance) and provides short-term loans to countries facing balance-of-payments crises. Demands strict economic reform packages (SAPs).
*   **World Bank**: Promotes long-term economic development and poverty reduction by financing infrastructure projects (roads, schools, dams).
*   **Asian Development Bank (ADB)**: Regional institution established in 1966 to finance infrastructure and reduce poverty in the Asia-Pacific region.
*   **1997 Asian Financial Crisis Case Study**: Triggered by Thailand floating the Baht due to excessive USD short-term debt. Contagion spread across East Asia. The IMF provided $110 billion in rescue loans under strict austerity and structural reform conditionalities.

## 🔑 SECTION 2: MEMORY ACROBATS & STUDY AIDS

### 💡 Memory Hook: IMF vs. World Bank vs. ADB — "MIA"
*   **M** — **M**onetary Stability (IMF: Short-term crisis loans & policy advice)
*   **I** — **I**nfrastructure (World Bank: Long-term global development & poverty loans)
*   **A** — **A**sia-Pacific (ADB: Regional development, based in Manila)

### 💡 Memory Hook: Forex Exposure Triad — "T T E"
*   **Transaction Exposure** — Actual cash flow risk on outstanding invoices (Short-term).
*   **Translation Exposure** — Accounting paper risk when consolidating financial statements (Reporting date).
*   **Economic Exposure** — Long-term competitive threat to future cash flows (Strategic).

### 💡 Derivative Comparison Matrix
*   **Forwards**: OTC, customized, obligated to trade, $0 upfront fee.
*   **Futures**: Exchange-traded, standardized, obligated to trade, margin deposits required.
*   **Options**: Exchange or OTC, standard/custom, **no obligation to trade** (buyer has choice), upfront **Premium** fee.
*   **Swaps**: Exchanging principal and interest in different currencies to reduce borrowing costs.

## 🎯 EXAM TIPS FOR 100% MARKS

### ✅ How to Tackle Forex Bid-Ask Spread Numericals
1.  **Extract the Quote**: Given a quote like `USD/INR = 83.40 / 83.60`:
    *   Identify the left number as the **Bid Rate** (Rate at which the bank *buys* USD).
    *   Identify the right number as the **Ask Rate** (Rate at which the bank *sells* USD).
2.  **Calculate Currency Spread**:
    $$\text{Spread} = \text{Ask Rate} - \text{Bid Rate}$$
3.  **Calculate Spread %**:
    $$\text{Spread \%} = \frac{\text{Ask Rate} - \text{Bid Rate}}{\text{Ask Rate}} \times 100$$
4.  **State the Implication**: A wider spread means lower liquidity and higher transaction costs for buyers.

### ✅ Common Exam Mistakes to Avoid
*   ❌ **Confusing Bid and Ask Rates**: Remember: the bank always **buys low** (Bid) and **sells high** (Ask). From the customer's perspective, you sell at the bid rate and buy at the ask rate.
*   ❌ **Confusing IMF and World Bank Roles**: Do not say the IMF funds road or power grid construction. The World Bank (IBRD) funds projects; the IMF fixes currency/monetary crises.
*   ❌ **Option Buyers Obligation**: Never state that currency option buyers *must* execute the contract. They can walk away and trade in the spot market if the spot rate is more favorable.
*   ❌ **Treating Translation Exposure as Cash Loss**: Do not say translation exposure hurts daily cash flow. It is a paper adjustment on financial statements, not a realized cash loss.
