## 🔑 SECTION 1: KEY POINTS — CORE CURRICULUM

### ▶ International Trade Fundamentals
*   **Definition**: The cross-border exchange of goods, services, and capital.
*   **Need**: Driven by differences in resources, technology, skills, and the need for resource efficiency.
*   **Benefits**: Market expansion, resource optimization, economic growth, tech transfer, and product variety.

### ▶ Mercantilism & Limitations
*   **Core Idea**: Wealth is gold/silver. Accumulate it via trade surplus ($Exports > Imports$).
*   **Government Role**: Restrict imports with tariffs/quotas, subsidize local manufacturing and exports.
*   **Limitations**: Creates zero-sum games, encourages trade wars, ignores productivity, and causes colonizer-colonized conflicts.

### ▶ Theory of Absolute Advantage (Adam Smith, 1776)
*   **Core Idea**: Country specializes in producing goods using **fewer resources/labor** than others.
*   **Trade Logic**: Specialize where absolutely efficient; trade for other goods. Mutual benefit.
*   **Examples**: Brazil (coffee), Germany (cars), China (electronics/Saudi oil).

### ▶ Theory of Comparative Advantage (David Ricardo, 1817)
*   **Core Idea**: Specialize where you have the **lowest opportunity cost** (relative efficiency).
*   **Significance**: Explains why trade benefits even if a country is less efficient in everything.
*   **Example**: India specializing in IT services due to its large skilled workforce.

### ▶ Factor Mobility Theory
*   **Core Idea**: Factors (labor, capital, technology) move across borders seeking higher yields.
*   **Impact**: Offsets or replaces trade (e.g., Apple building factories in China instead of exporting from the US).

### ▶ Regional Economic Integration
*   **Definition**: Agreements among regional countries to reduce/eliminate trade barriers.
*   **Benefits**: Increased trade, market expansion, efficiency, competition, and political cooperation.

### ▶ WTO & European Union
*   **WTO**: Established 1995 to enforce free, fair, non-discriminatory trade rules. Successor to GATT.
*   **EU**: 27-nation single market with common tariffs and currency (Euro) to promote European economic integration.

## 🔑 SECTION 2: MEMORY ACROBATS & STUDY AIDS

### 💡 Memory Hook: 6 Features of Globalization — "I F I T G C"
*   **I** — Integration of Global Markets
*   **F** — Free Flow of Goods and Services
*   **I** — International Movement of Capital
*   **T** — Technological Advancement
*   **G** — Growth of MNCs
*   **C** — Cultural Exchange and Connectivity

### 💡 Porter's Diamond Model Mnemonic — "F D R S"
*   **F** — Factor Conditions (Basic vs. Advanced)
*   **D** — Demand Conditions (Sophisticated domestic buyers)
*   **R** — Rivalry (Intense domestic competition)
*   **S** — Supporting Industries (Domestic suppliers/clusters)

### 💡 5 Levels of Economic Integration Ladder
```
                     [ 5. Political Union  ]  <-- USA (Full unification)
                  [ 4. Economic Union   ]  <-- Eurozone (Shared currency & policy)
               [ 3. Common Market    ]  <-- EAC (Free labor/capital movement)
            [ 2. Customs Union    ]  <-- MERCOSUR (Shared external tariff)
         [ 1. Free Trade Area  ]  <-- USMCA (Internal tariffs removed)
```

## 🎯 EXAM TIPS FOR 100% MARKS

### ✅ How to Tackle Trade Theory Numericals
1.  **State the Definition**: Write a 2-line description of Comparative Advantage and Opportunity Cost.
2.  **State the Formula**: Write out $\text{OC of Good X} = \frac{\text{Good Y given up}}{\text{Good X gained}}$ clearly.
3.  **Draw the Table**: Show your opportunity cost calculations in a structured comparison table.
4.  **Highlight the Choice**: Bold the country that has the *lowest* opportunity cost for each good.
5.  **Calculate the Terms of Trade (ToT)**: Choose a trading price between the opportunity costs and explain how both countries benefit.

### ✅ Common Exam Mistakes to Avoid
*   ❌ **Zero-Sum vs. Positive-Sum Confusion**: Do not say Ricardo's theory is a zero-sum game. Mercantilism is zero-sum; Smith's and Ricardo's theories are **positive-sum** (both win).
*   ❌ **Ignoring Advanced Factors**: When discussing Porter's Diamond, students often focus only on natural resources. Remember that **Advanced Factors** (R&D, engineers) are far more critical than Basic Factors.
*   ❌ **Confusing FTA and Customs Union**: Make sure you distinguish them. In an FTA, members set their own external tariffs individually. In a Customs Union, they share a **common external tariff**.
*   ❌ **Skipping Corporate Examples**: Never write an abstract trade theory answer without mentioning real companies (e.g., Apple in China for factor mobility, BMW for Porter's Diamond).
