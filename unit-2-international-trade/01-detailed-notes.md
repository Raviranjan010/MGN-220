# Unit 2 — International Trade: Detailed Notes

## Topic: International Trade Theories
- **Mercantilism**: Countries should maximize exports and minimize imports to accumulate gold/silver. Zero-sum game.
- **Absolute Advantage (Adam Smith)**: Specializing in goods a country produces more efficiently than any other country.
- **Comparative Advantage (David Ricardo)**: Specializing in goods produced at the lowest opportunity cost.
- **Heckscher-Ohlin Theory (Factor Proportions)**: Exporting goods that utilize abundant domestic factors (e.g. labor, capital).
- **Porter's Diamond Model**: Factor conditions, Demand conditions, Supporting industries, and Firm Strategy/Rivalry.

## Topic: Regional Economic Integration
- **Levels**:
  1. Free Trade Area (NAFTA/USMCA)
  2. Customs Union (MERCOSUR)
  3. Common Market (Factors move freely)
  4. Economic Union (Eurozone - single currency)
  5. Political Union (USA).

## Topic: Regulatory Bodies
- **WTO**: Originating from GATT, it regulates global trade disputes.
- **European Union (EU)**: Single market, Eurozone currency, and Brexit impacts.
